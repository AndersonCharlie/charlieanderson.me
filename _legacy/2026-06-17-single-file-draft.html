01 Case study

Meal JOY

A cold-start meal-delivery brand taken to 4.43× ROAS on a lean daily budget — with a premium angle, in a category that mostly competes on price.

The case below is a food brand. The system under it — the math, the offer, the paid acquisition, the reorder engine — is the same one I run for local service and high-ticket clients.

The situation

Meal JOY is a chef-led meal-prep delivery business in Connecticut. Great food, loyal early customers, and no real paid acquisition. Growth was capped by whoever happened to hear about it.

What I did

I built the Meta (Facebook + Instagram) program from scratch: wrote the offer, built the creative, and ran a premium angle aimed squarely at the local, affluent market.

Then I managed it the way it should be managed. Small daily budget, a tight read on cost-per-acquisition and ROAS, constant creative testing. The winning image ads ended up running at about a third of the cost-per-click of the originals.

Here's what matters: a first order is just the start. Those customers reorder for months, so a campaign that looks like break-even on day one turns into real backend profit.

03 About

Who you're working with

I'm Charlie. I care about exactly one number on your end: how much more money you make. Everything else — the ads, the funnels, the emails — is just how we get there.

I'm an operator, not a theorist. I'd rather run a small budget profitably and scale what works than burn a launch budget on a "brand campaign" and hope. That means starting with the math: what a customer is worth, what you can pay to get one. Then building the offer before the ads ever go live.

I work done-for-you, so you're not learning ad managers at midnight, and I'm comfortable putting my pay on performance. Based in Fairfield County, Connecticut, working with businesses that have something genuinely good to sell.
